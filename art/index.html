Welcome to Artspace!

Artspace is a website dedicated to exploring the world of art and design. Here, you can learn about the elements and principles of design, as well as color theory. Whether you're an aspiring artist or just someone who appreciates art, Artspace has something for everyone.





























Elements of Design

The elements of design are the basic units of a visual image. They include line, shape, form, space, and texture as well as colour and value.


Line

Line is a straight or curved mark that connects two points or is the path of a moving point. Lines can be vertical, horizontal, diagonal, or curved. They can be any texture and can even be actual or implied.


Shape

Shape is a 2D area that is defined in some way. Shapes can be organic or geometric.

  • Organic shapes: Shapes made by the forces of nature. The opposite of manufactured shapes.
  • Geometric shapes: Precise shapes that can be described using mathematical formulas.

Form

Objects having three dimensions. Forms can also be organic or geometric.


Space

Space is the distances and areas around, between, and within components of a design. There are two kinds of space:

  • Positive space: Space that is occupied by a shape or form.
  • Negative space: Space that is not filled or occupied.

Texture

How things feel or look as if they might feel. Texture can be actual or implied.


Color

The element of art derived from reflected light. Color can be organized into color schemes.


Value

Value refers to the lightness or darkness of an object or color.


Principles of Design

The way elements are organized to create different visual effects. It helps create meaning, add interest, and help the artist achieve their desired outcome.


Rhythm/Movement

Create the look and feeling of action and guide the viewer's eyes throughout the artwork. Movement can be visual or kinetic. Indicates movement by the repetition of elements or objects.


Emphasis

The part of an artwork that is dominant over other parts. Emphasis can be created by placement of the object on the picture plane or by isolation or contrast.


Unity

Unity is when all parts of an artwork work together to create a sense of wholeness and completeness. Usually done by stressing the similarities of separate but related elements.


Variety

Variety is the use of different or contrasting elements to create interest. It is the opposite of unity.


Repetition

Repetition is the use of the same or similar elements throughout an artwork to create unity and visual interest.


Pattern

Pattern is the repetition of a design element in a regular arrangement. It can be used to create rhythm and movement.


Balance

Balance is the distribution of visual weight in an artwork. It can be symmetrical, asymmetrical, or radial.

Color Theory

Color theory is a body of practical guidance to color mixing and the visual effects of a specific color combination. It includes the color wheel, color schemes, and the psychological effects of colors.


Color Wheel

A circular diagram of colors arranged by their chromatic relationship. The most common color wheel is the 12-color wheel, which includes primary, secondary, and tertiary colors.


Color Properties

Color has 6 main properties: hue, value, tint, shade, intensity, and tone.

Hue

Hue is the name of a color. (ex. red, blue, green)


Value

Value is the lightness or darkness of a color. It can be changed by adding white or black to the color.

  • Tint: Lighter values made by adding white to a color.
  • Shade: Darker values made by adding black to a color.

Intensity

How bright or dull a color appears. Made by adding its opposite/complementary.


Tone

Created by adding grey.


Color Schemes

A color scheme is a combination of colors that are used together in a design. Some common color schemes include complementary, analogous, triadic, and monochromatic.
